# The Realm of the Forgotten Crown — World Almanac

## The World

The Realm is a high-fantasy world in decline. An ancient kingdom once united the lands under a single crown — the Crown of Unity, forged by the first mage-king to bind peace through magic rather than force. When the kingdom fell to civil war, the Crown was shattered into five pieces and scattered across the realm. Without it, the land fractured into city-states, wilderness, and ruin.

## The Crown

The Forgotten Crown (Crown of Unity) is the central artifact of the world. Five shards, each holding a fragment of the original enchantment:

1. **The Shard of Clarity** — said to grant understanding of any language, code, or cipher
2. **The Shard of Binding** — can forge unbreakable agreements between parties
3. **The Shard of Sight** — allows the bearer to see truth through any illusion
4. **The Shard of Passage** — opens any locked door, gate, or barrier
5. **The Shard of Memory** — preserves knowledge against destruction or decay

Reassembling the Crown is the endgame quest. Whether it should be reassembled — or whether some things are better left broken — is the central moral question.

## Key Locations

### Thornwick Village (Starting Zone)
A modest trading town at the crossroads of three regions. Home to farmers, merchants, and adventurers passing through. The Shattered Tankard tavern serves as the social hub. The Temple of the Dawn offers healing. Captain Hendricks guards the south gate and posts bounties.

### The Whispering Woods (Level 5-10)
An ancient forest east of Thornwick. The trees whisper — some say it's the wind, others say it's the forest remembering. Home to wolves, forest spirits, and a druid circle that distrusts outsiders. A ruined elven outpost lies deep within.

### Ironridge Mountains (Level 10-15)
Rocky peaks west of Thornwick. The dwarven mines once produced the finest iron in the realm. Now the deep shafts are abandoned — something drove the miners out. Kobolds have set up camp in the northern pass, harassing caravans.

### The Open Plains (Level 8-12)
Grasslands south of Thornwick, dotted with ruins of the old kingdom. Wandering mercenaries, bandits, and the occasional patrol from a distant city-state. A watchtower ruin marks the entrance to the first Crown Shard dungeon.

### The Sunken Crypts (Level 15-20) — NEW, UNRELEASED
An underground zone accessible through a hidden entrance in the Whispering Woods. Three boss encounters, puzzle rooms, and the second Crown Shard. Currently in testing.

### The Rusty Dwarf Inn (Planned)
A remote waystation deep in the Ironridge Mountains, run by a surly dwarf who serves ale so strong it counts as a potion. A rest point for miners and adventurers heading into the deep shafts. Rumored to have a basement fighting ring. *Not yet fleshed out — needs rooms, NPCs, and quest hooks.*

### The Fields of Standing Stones (Planned)
Vast open fields east of the Open Plains, dominated by ancient standing stones that predate the old kingdom. The stones hum with a magic nobody understands. Local farmers avoid them at night. A potential zone for mid-level exploration and a Crown Shard quest. *Not yet fleshed out — needs rooms, NPCs, and environmental storytelling.*

### The Torse Fields (Planned)
An arena district where the sport of Torse is played. *Not yet fleshed out — needs rules, arena rooms, NPC players/referees, and spectator areas. What even IS Torse? Ask Tyraziel.*

### The Advisor's Sanctum (Hidden)
A secret room accessible through a false wall in the Temple of the Dawn. Home to The Advisor, an ancient figure who holds the Signed Tome of MUD Programming. Players who find him can accept a quest to deliver the Tome to Tyraziel.

## Factions

### The Dawn Temple
Clerics and paladins devoted to preservation and healing. Neutral in political conflicts. Will help anyone who asks.

### The Ironridge Miners' Guild
Dwarves who want their mines back. Hiring adventurers to deal with whatever lurks in the deep shafts. Pay well.

### The Whispering Circle
Druids who protect the forest. Suspicious of outsiders but will trade knowledge for service. Know things about the Crown that others have forgotten.

### The Shadow Wolves (Player Guild)
A PvP-focused guild that controls territory in the northern wilderness. Aggressive, organized, and controversial. Currently in a feud with the Blood Ravens.

### The Blood Ravens (Player Guild)
A PvE-focused guild that accuses the Shadow Wolves of exploiting PvP zone boundaries. The rivalry is the community's biggest ongoing drama.

## Tone

The world is serious but not grim. There's danger and darkness, but also warmth — the tavern, the innkeeper's laugh, the priestess's quiet strength. The humor comes from the characters, not from the world mocking itself. Players should feel like they're in a real place with real stakes, even if the stakes are about goblin bounties and enchanted copper rings.

## Design Principles

1. **Every room tells a story.** Room descriptions should evoke atmosphere, not just list furniture.
2. **NPCs have inner lives.** Even the guard captain has opinions, fears, and a retirement plan.
3. **Quests have choices.** The best quests present genuine dilemmas, not just "kill 10 goblins."
4. **The world responds.** Player actions should have consequences that ripple through the realm.
5. **Text is the medium.** We chose text because it respects the player's imagination. Honor that choice.
6. **Open formats.** World data should migrate to NRSP (Narrative RPG Save Point Format) — rooms as Location Sheets, NPCs as Character Sheets, secrets as NPC Sheets. The game's content should be as open and readable as its code.
