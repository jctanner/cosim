## Acceptance Criteria: "Successfully Transitioned" (All Products)

A product has successfully transitioned when:

✅ **Velocity Improvement Demonstrated**
- Measurable reduction in cycle time (feature ideation → production)
- Increased release frequency without quality degradation
- Faster response to customer feature requests

✅ **Quality Standards Maintained**
- Defect escape rate holds or improves vs. baseline
- Customer support ticket volume does not increase
- CSAT/NPS scores hold or improve

✅ **Revenue Impact Validated**
- Deal win rates improve in velocity-sensitive opportunities (Wave 2)
- At-risk ARR retention improves (Waves 2 & 3)
- Expansion revenue accelerates (faster feature delivery drives upsell)
- Churn risk reduced (customer satisfaction with responsiveness)

✅ **Team Operating Model Stabilized**
- Engineers effectively orchestrating agents (not just prompting)
- Quality gates automated and enforced
- Documentation and support keeping pace with dev velocity

### Risk Mitigation

**Coordination Overhead Rollback Plan:**
If coordination overhead materializes in Week 1-2 (symptoms: excessive merge conflicts, divergent agent patterns, quality gate conflicts), execute rollback:
- Pull Quality/Testing engineer to Wave 4+ or other priority
- Operate as 4-engineer team (2 backend, 2 frontend)
- Absorb testing responsibility into backend/frontend pods
- Cost impact: ~$4-5K wasted capacity vs. $15-20K if we wait until Week 6

**Success Indicators (by Week 2):**
- Engineers working independently within their workstreams
- Quality gates passing without cross-team conflicts
- Contract schemas enabling clean integration
- Minimal merge conflicts or pattern divergence

If success indicators aren't met by Week 2, trigger rollback immediately.
