## Decision Framework

### Go/No-Go Criteria for Wave 2

**Measure at Week 6 of Wave 1 (Ansible):**

✅ **PROCEED to Wave 2 if:**
- PR cycle time reduced by ≥30%
- Feature velocity increased by ≥50%
- No major quality degradation (defect escape rate stable or improved)
- Actual costs within 20% of plan

🛑 **PAUSE Wave 2 if:**
- Velocity improvements <20%
- Quality degradation (defect escape rate +50% or higher)
- Costs exceed plan by >30%
- Customer satisfaction declining

### Go/No-Go Criteria for Wave 3

**Measure at Week 10 (mid-Wave 2):**

✅ **PROCEED to Wave 3 if:**
- Wave 2 showing ≥30% velocity improvement
- Quality metrics stable or improved
- Costs within 20% of plan
- No major execution issues with 4-engineer team

🛑 **PAUSE Wave 3 if:**
- Wave 2 velocity improvements <20%
- Quality degradation in Wave 2
- Costs significantly over plan
- Organizational capacity strained

### Wave 3 Team Size Rollback Criteria

**Measure at Week 1-2 of Wave 3 (EARLY detection):**

🔄 **ROLLBACK to 4 engineers if:**
- Coordination overhead visible (excessive merge conflicts, duplicated work)
- Velocity per engineer declining vs. Wave 2 baseline
- Team reports communication/dependency bottlenecks
- Cost of coordination exceeding value of 5th engineer

**Financial impact of rollback:** ~$4-5K wasted (2 weeks × 1 engineer). This is acceptable experimental cost vs. $800K at stake.

### Scale-Up Criteria for Wave 4+

**Measure at Week 20 (end of Wave 2 + mid-Wave 3):**

✅ **SCALE to all products if:**
- All waves showing ≥50% velocity improvement
- Revenue impact validated (closed ≥1 deal on faster integration timeline, retained ≥50% of at-risk ARR)
- ROI tracking >2.5x
- Teams report sustainable workflows (not burning out)

## Risk Factors

**Financial Risks:**
1. **Costs exceed estimates** — If capacity reduction lasts longer than 6 weeks, costs compound
2. **Revenue upside doesn't materialize** — Velocity improvements don't translate to deal wins
3. **Quality degradation causes churn** — Faster shipping of broken features increases support costs and churn
4. **Competitive catch-up** — Competitors also adopt agentic workflows, neutralizing our advantage
5. **Wave 3 coordination overhead** — 5-engineer team creates Brooks' Law bottlenecks instead of velocity gains

**Mitigation:**
- Hard stop/reassess at Week 6 of each wave
- Tie sales comp to velocity-driven deal wins (incentivize Taylor's team to prove revenue impact)
- Quality gates are non-negotiable (Alex's testing infrastructure is table stakes)
- Track NPS/CSAT religiously — if customer satisfaction drops, pause transformation
- **NEW: Fast rollback on Wave 3 team size (Week 1-2 assessment, not Week 6) to minimize wasted cost**
